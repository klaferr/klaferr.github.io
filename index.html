About me

I am a second year Ph.D. student at Purdue University working with Professor Ali Bramson. I am broadly interested in combining observations and modeling to explore planetary ices and their variability. My current research involves mapping the subsurface migration troughs in Mars' northern polar cap using the 3D SHARAD dataset.

Previously, I earned by B.S. in Astronomy and Physics at University of Maryland, College Park. There, I worked with Professor Jessica Sunshine and Dr. Lori Feaga exploring the variation of the 3 micron hydration feature across the southern hemisphere of the Moon. For more about my past and present research, check out my Projects page.

I care deeply about creating a field and world that is more equitable and accessible. For resources and more information on these effors, check out my Outreach, Teaching, and DEI page.

My pronouns are she/her/hers and they/them/theirs
